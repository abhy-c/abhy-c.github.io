# Overview

This project is a premium personal portfolio site for Abhyudai Chauhan, a product manager with an NYU MBA (STEM), ex-attorney background, and a focus on AI, SQL, fintech, and regulated systems. The experience should feel credible, calm, strategic, and business-ready rather than trendy, startup-generic, playful, or overly minimal to the point of feeling empty.

The site is content-forward and trust-forward. It should help a first-time visitor understand who the site owner is, what problems he works on, and what action to take next within a few seconds. Prioritize clarity, polish, restraint, and information hierarchy over novelty for novelty's sake.

# Brand Intent

- Communicate executive polish without feeling stiff or corporate-template generic.
- Signal cross-functional depth: product strategy, legal judgment, data-informed execution, AI literacy, and fintech credibility.
- Feel bespoke and editorial, with enough warmth to support consulting conversion.
- Keep the emotional tone calm, assured, intelligent, and deliberate.

# Product Context

- Site type: personal portfolio and consulting website
- Primary audience:
  - hiring managers and recruiters for product roles
  - founders and operators evaluating advisory or consulting fit
  - peers reading long-form writing for signal and credibility
- Primary goals:
  - establish professional credibility quickly
  - present selected projects and writing with high signal density
  - create a simple path to book a free consult
  - preserve a low-maintenance, static-content operating model

# Visual Direction

- Use a refined editorial-business aesthetic.
- Prefer soft warmth in the background, crisp dark text, and carefully rationed accent color.
- The overall feeling should be closer to a premium strategy memo or well-designed annual report than a SaaS landing page.
- Use generous whitespace, rounded cards, and clear rhythm between sections.
- Avoid clutter, dashboard density, flashy gradients, glassmorphism overload, neon, or playful illustration-heavy motifs.

# Typography

- Primary sans: `"Avenir Next", "Avenir", "Helvetica Neue", sans-serif`
- Secondary serif: `"Iowan Old Style", "Palatino Linotype", "Book Antiqua", Georgia, serif`

Typography rules:

- Headings should feel decisive, tight, and modern.
- Use strong negative tracking on major headlines.
- Serif is used sparingly for sophistication and contrast, especially for kicker text, quotes, or emphasis lines.
- Body copy should stay highly readable, with comfortable line length and enough vertical rhythm for long-form reading.
- Avoid trendy display fonts, monospaced-heavy design systems, or hyper-condensed editorial treatments.

# Layout Principles

- Build around large rounded content cards on a warm page background.
- Use section spacing that feels measured and breathable.
- Favor centered page containers with consistent horizontal padding.
- On desktop, use asymmetric editorial grids where helpful, especially in hero sections.
- On mobile, preserve hierarchy and card rhythm without reducing everything to a bland stacked list.

Spacing and structure:

- hero sections should feel prominent and immediately legible
- cards should have enough padding to feel premium
- transitions between sections should be obvious through spacing and contrast, not heavy dividers
- long-form pages should support comfortable reading first, decoration second

# Component Patterns

## Navigation

- Keep navigation clean, restrained, and obvious.
- Labels should be simple and professional: Home, About, Projects, Writing, Consulting.
- Do not overcomplicate the header with secondary menus or utility clutter.

## Hero Sections

- Start with a small uppercase eyebrow in teal.
- Use a large headline with strong hierarchy.
- Follow with a concise credibility-rich summary and one supporting paragraph.
- Primary CTA should be direct and low-friction.
- Secondary CTA can point to writing, projects, or resume.

## Cards

- Cards are a core motif across homepage, projects, blog index, and consulting modules.
- Use rounded corners generously.
- Borders should be soft and visible.
- Shadows should be present but understated.
- Cards should feel polished and tactile, not heavy or aggressively elevated.

## Content Lists

- Blog and project collections should feel curated rather than infinite-feed oriented.
- Emphasize title, summary, category, and reason-to-click.
- Keep metadata useful but visually quiet.

## Long-form Content

- Long-form reading experience should feel editorial and trustworthy.
- Preserve comfortable line height.
- Use clear heading hierarchy.
- Blockquotes should use the serif voice and a teal left border.
- Inline code and separators should be subtle and disciplined.

## Consulting CTA

- Maintain one clear launch offer: a free consult.
- The CTA should feel professional and calm, not salesy.
- Booking modules should appear intentional even when the live Calendly embed is not active.

# Imagery

- Use the professional headshot as a grounding human signal on the About page and where appropriate.
- The NYC skyline image can be used as atmosphere, not as the primary brand.
- Imagery should reinforce seriousness, polish, and place without becoming decorative filler.
- Avoid stock-photo energy, cartoon illustrations, abstract AI blobs, or generic startup iconography.

# Motion and Interaction

- Motion should be restrained, useful, and polished.
- Small hover lifts and gentle transitions are appropriate.
- Motion should support clarity and refinement, not spectacle.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Avoid dramatic parallax, spring-heavy animation, or distracting auto-motion.

# Voice and Content Tone

- Voice should be intelligent, direct, and measured.
- Copy should feel like a thoughtful operator speaking clearly, not a marketer performing expertise.
- Avoid buzzword stacking, motivational fluff, founder-bro energy, or hype-driven AI language.
- Good themes: ambiguity reduction, decision quality, operating systems, measurable learning, product judgment, stakeholder clarity.

# Reusable UI Cues

- small uppercase eyebrow labels with generous tracking
- oversized headlines with tight tracking
- shell-like cards with warm white gradients and subtle borders
- teal as the primary functional accent
- bronze as a selective supporting accent
- serif support text to create editorial contrast

# Accessibility and UX Constraints

- Maintain strong text contrast.
- Keep tap targets comfortable on mobile.
- Preserve clear heading structure.
- Avoid using color alone to communicate important state.
- Maintain readability over visual cleverness.

# Anti-Patterns

- no generic venture-backed SaaS look
- no purple-heavy AI aesthetic
- no overly dark or cyberpunk themes
- no excessive glassmorphism
- no playful consumer-app illustrations
- no cluttered dashboards on portfolio pages
- no vague copy that hides the actual professional profile

# Build Guidance For Stitch

- When generating variants, preserve the current tone before exploring larger departures.
- If redesigning, improve hierarchy and compositional confidence without changing the underlying professional positioning.
- Prefer better layout, spacing, and refinement over introducing entirely new visual metaphors.
- If a design choice conflicts with clarity or credibility, choose clarity and credibility.
